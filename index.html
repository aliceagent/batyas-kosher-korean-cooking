The Workshop

A Cozy Korean Cooking Night, Made Kosher

Three hands-on hours · all levels welcome · come solo, as a date night, or with a group of friends.

Spend three hands-on hours learning to cook beloved Korean dishes from scratch — crispy pancakes, sizzling bulgogi, glossy japchae and sweet molten hotteok. Every recipe is 100% kosher, beginner-friendly, and made with ingredients you can find right here in Israel. Come on your own to learn something new and meet people, bring someone for a wonderful date night, or book in with a group of friends.

The Traditions

A Taste of Korean Food Culture

The warmth and traditions behind the dishes you'll make.

Banchan 반찬

Little side dishes shared around the table — everyone reaches in together. There's no "starter then main"; the table simply fills up, and the joy is in the variety and the company.

Bap 밥

Rice sits at the centre of the meal — so central that a common Korean greeting, "Have you eaten rice?", really means "Are you well?".

Kimchi & Fermentation 김장

Fermentation is the soul of the cuisine — kimchi and the trio of jang. The autumn ritual of kimjang is UNESCO cultural heritage. You'll take home your own jar.

Balance 오방색

Cooks chase balance — the five colours of obangsaek make a plate both beautiful and nourishing, while seasonings harmonise salty, sweet, sour, bitter and spicy.

Jeong 정

Underlying it all is jeong — a warm affection expressed by feeding people generously. Cooking it yourself is the best way to taste why Korean food has won the world over.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any cooking experience?
Not at all. Every class is beginner-friendly and fully guided, step by step, at your own station. All levels are welcome.
Is everything really kosher?
Yes — every dish is strictly kosher and clearly labelled Meat or Pareve, never mixed. We swap non-kosher Korean staples cleanly: soy sauce for fish sauce, kosher-certified gochujang and gochugaru, and no anchovy stock.
Is there any pork or shellfish?
Never. There is no pork and no shellfish in any dish, sauce or stock.
Are the dishes meat or pareve?
Each dish is clearly marked. Most mains also have a vegetarian / pareve option (for example, mushroom-tofu bulgogi or a tofu version of the gochujang "chicken"). Tell us about allergies in advance and we'll guide your choices.
Where is the class held?
In Hashmonaim, Israel. The exact street address is shared after you sign up via the WhatsApp group.
How do I book?
Just join the WhatsApp group — that's the only place we post class dates and open spots. There are no sign-up forms.
How long is the workshop, and what does it cost?
It runs about 3 hours and costs 350 NIS per person. You can pay with Bit, Paybox or cash.
Can I come alone?
Absolutely. It's great solo — a relaxed way to learn something new and meet people — and just as lovely as a date night or with a group of friends.
Is it good for a date night or a group?
Yes to both. The hands-on, cook-and-share format is perfect for couples and for groups of friends. Bring whoever you like.
What about drinks and spice level?
Soft drinks, beer and saké are available to purchase separately at dinner. Spice is adjustable — the cucumber salad, dak-galbi, braised tofu and your kimchi can be as mild or fiery as you like, and bibimbap's gochujang goes on to taste. Everything else is gentle.
